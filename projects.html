Deployment of nutrient sensors to measure pollution
in the North Sea - Data Analysis

This project involved deployment of 'lab on chip' nitrate and phosphate sensors on an underway system on a fisheries vessel.
Following data collection (including validation datasets), an automated data QC process was developed in Matlab and the curated data corrected for tidal flow direction and correlated to regions of freshwater influence.
The data shows that spikes in nutrient pollution are located where the salinity ("saltiness") of the sea is lower, where water input from rivers is higher.
This shows that the sources of high nutrients in the North Sea on this day were from land rather than ocean - it is likely human-generated pollution.
